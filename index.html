Hi, I'm Renata Guerra!

I'm half Czech but born and raised in Venezuela and now living in London. I decided to move to the UK due to the current political situation in my home country, which made me leave behind my dream of becoming an Engineer, but luckily, London has been an amazing city full of opportunities (like FAC) for a passionate woman in tech, like me!

To start up my life in the UK, I found a part-time job as a nanny which allowed me enough time to explore the world of coding and boost my interest in technology. I started doing online courses at home... Since then, I really engaged with coding as I consider myself an eager learner person, I love learning and coding really suits me as its a never-ending learning process which really excites me!

After a couple of months of teaching myself the basics, I joined CodeFirst:Girls courses (which I'll tell you a bit more later...) here's where I realised that becoming a developer is exactly what I want - and from then, I applied for this amazing program as I believe its the best opportunity for me to tackle a career in tech, while being in a supportive environment and being able to grow with and within FAC.

WHY FAC?

My principal motivation to have applied to Founders & Coders is to optimise my inquisitive curiosity about web and software development. I truly believe this is an authentic opportunity for me to develop my expertise and build up my career in a collaborative team environment. Delivering my fresh ideas and creativity to ensure that I can contribute efficiently to Founders & Coders mission and future with the help of previous fellows sounds like the best of the chances to explore the industry and settle down in a suitable role in an outstanding team, all of this, after gaining the experience "hands-on" throughout the 18 weeks at FAC.

Another of my biggest reasons to choose FAC over other bootcamps it's because here I'll not only learn web development, but also, will join an amazing community to make a difference. I see FAC as an open door to everyone, excluding their backgrounds and experiences, FAC as a free bootcamp is an ideal place for me to finally feel that someone believes in my potential and willingness to learn, and not in my non-existing degree or experience elsewhere.

Becoming part of FAC means the world to me, being surrounded by people who have the same interests and similar goals as me, is a wonderful way of encouraging each other to succeed. After being teaching myself how to code at home, I now understand the importance of interacting with other people, sharing and receiving help, making projects in teams, communicating, all these and so much more is what actually makes a great developer, and FAC is the perfect place to find all of these and more!
